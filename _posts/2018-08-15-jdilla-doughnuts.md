Doughnuts is such an incredible album.
Not because it is high quality, but because of the circumstances it was created in.
J Dilla knew he was going to die.
He had an incurable blood disease that was slowing wearing him away during his last years.
Despite his illness, he still toured Europe, performing in a wheelchair.

Even on his last days, J Dilla never stopped working on his music, and Doughnuts is an album that makes you fully aware that it was born on a deathbed.
An incredible use of sampling as been used to paint a picture of loss, either relating directly, or being twisted slightly to change their message.
My favourite example of this is on ["Workinonit"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5nO7IA1DeeI), where a sample feels like it's been tweaked to sound like a mix of "fade me", and "save me".
